Areas of concern
1. SUPPLIER DELIVERY DELAYS (VAGB - PLATES)
Observation: We have noted a recovery in the release of plates by VAGB this week, following a period of decreased productivity initiated at the start of 2026. Please refer to the VA EEW Scheduled Delivery documentation for specific figures.
Impact: A sustained downward trend in weekly deliveries persists for all PO items. Although VAGB forecasts an imminent ramp-up, the previous shortfall in capacity has caused a two-week slippage against the VAGB Statement timeline.
Current Status: 3654 plates (81,16 %) delivered with reference to SAIPEM PO quantity of 4502 pieces ( -18,84%)
Action Plan: Focus on expediting plate deliveries and increase coordination with VAGB to ensure timely fulfilment of the remaining quantities.
2. RELEASE OF LINE PIPES (EEW - LINE PIPES)
Concern: The project is progressing, though at a significantly slower rate in comparison to the previous months.
Impact: The timely release of line pipes is critical for the construction of offshore riser platforms and pipelines. Delayed releases could halt subsequent activities such as welding, coating, and pipeline assembly.
Current Status: The current release rate is in accordance with production, with an average discrepancy of 2,6 % for the pipes inspected and released of the PO.
There appears to be some confusion regarding the PO quantity from EEW as the manufacturer. As discussed in multiple conversations with them, the IQS dataset provided on behalf of SAIPEM does not align with EEW’s records. The dataset reflects the SAIPEM PO, while EEW’s references correspond to the SUMITOMO PO. On 10.04.2026, the writer received the following Purchase Orders:
Purchase Order No. 1506050 Rev. 02 dated 12.03.2026
Purchase Order No. 1519921 Rev. 02 dated 12.03.2026
Purchase Order No. 1541227 dated 12.03.2026
This discrepancy, along with the recent modification to the Purchase Orders, affects the calculation of IQS on behalf of SAIPEM, including daily and weekly production progress, order status, and all reporting related to the issued SAIPEM Purchase Orders.
Timeline: Regarding the SAIPEM POs, the current batch of line pipes is ahead of schedule, with the PO delivery planned accordingly. However, IQS is monitoring the potential order closure dates based on the production trend derived from EEW's data over the last 15 days (see above).
